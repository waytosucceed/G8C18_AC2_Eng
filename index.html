Contractions


Grammar overview:

Today we mainly focussed on the following:
Today we focussed on the following: Contractions are shortened combinations of two words.Vowels only or the consonant preceding a vowel and the vowel togetherly are dropped to cause the contraction. The consequent dropping of a vowel sound makes communication easier and faster, gives an informal touch to stories/creative writing or contributes to developing the rhyme and rhythm in poetry. Contractions are deemed inappropriate for official emails, formal letters and academic writing. An apostrophe is used to indicate a contraction when a letter is absent.
Contractions are made in the following pairs:

  1. Pronouns and Primary helping verbs
  2. Pronouns and Modals
  3. Primary Helping verbs and NOT
  4. Modals and NOT
  5. Modals and Primary helping Verbs
  6. Contractions can come after names, nouns, question words, and the words "here, there, and now."
  7. Transitive Verb and Pronoun: Exception!

There are exceptional cases where contractions do not occur at all.

Word meaning:
Creak: Verb-(of an object or structure, typically a wooden one) makes a harsh, high-pitched sound when being moved or when pressure or weight is applied.
Use: I heard the window creak.

Hinges: a movable joint or mechanism on which a door, gate, or lid swings as it opens and closes or which connects linked objects.
Use: TThe thief could break in because the hinges on this door were too rusty and weak.

Floorboards: a long plank making up part of a wooden floor in a building
Use: ince this is a damp area floorboards wouldn’t last long.
